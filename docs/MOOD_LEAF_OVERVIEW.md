## Core Philosophy

### The Mood Leaf Ethics

Mood Leaf follows strict ethical principles that guide everything we do:

#### 1. Never Diagnose
We will **never** label you with mental health conditions. Instead of "you have anxiety disorder," we say "you've been mentioning feeling anxious lately." Only qualified professionals can diagnoseâ€”we're not that.

#### 2. Never Prescribe
We will **never** tell you to start, stop, or change medications. Those decisions are between you and your doctor.

#### 3. Always Tentative
We use language like "it seems," "I wonder if," and "you might notice" because only YOU truly know your experience. We never tell you how you feel.

#### 4. Anti-Dependency
Mood Leaf is designed to help you **not need it**. We actively encourage:
- Real-world connections over app conversations
- Building your own coping skills
- Trusting your own wisdom
- Stepping away when appropriate

Our success is when you say "I don't need this app anymore."

#### 5. Privacy First
Everything stays on your device. We have no servers. We literally cannot see your data.

#### 6. Encourage Professional Help
For persistent struggles, we always encourage seeking professional support. We're a companion, not a replacement for therapy.

## Journaling (Leaves)

### The Leaf Philosophy

In Mood Leaf, journal entries are called **Leaves**. Like leaves on a tree, they're meant to be releasedâ€”noticed, acknowledged, and let go.

**Journaling is noticing, not archiving.**

A leaf is a single thought, word, or feeling:
- Short, not long
- Released, not saved
- Let go, not held onto

This protects you from the trap of endless reviewing and rumination.

### Creating Entries

#### Text Entry
1. Tap the **+** button on the home screen
2. Write your thoughts
3. Tap **Save**

#### Voice Entry
1. Tap the **+** button
2. Tap the **microphone icon**
3. Speak your entry
4. Review and edit if needed
5. Tap **Save**

### What Happens When You Save

When you save a leaf, Mood Leaf automatically:
- **Detects mood** - Positive, negative, or neutral with intensity
- **Extracts topics** - People, events, themes you mentioned
- **Updates your Tree** - Adds to your life context for better conversations
- **Finds significance** - Marks important entries that contain life context

### Entry Features

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Mood Detection** | Color-coded (green = positive, red = negative, gray = neutral) |
| **Topic Tags** | People, emotions, activities, life areas |
| **Talk About This** | Start a conversation about a specific entry |
| **Search & Filter** | Find entries by content, mood, or date |

## Talking to Your Guide (The Sprout)

### How Conversation Works

Your guide (The Sprout) doesn't just respond to your current messageâ€”it knows your whole story. Every conversation draws from:

- **Your journal entries** - Recent ones with full text
- **Your life context** - People, events, patterns from months of journaling
- **Your psychological patterns** - How you think, cope, and process
- **Your health data** - Sleep, activity, heart rate (if enabled)
- **Your preferences** - Communication style, what helps you most

### The Conversation Approach

Your guide follows a consistent approach:

1. **VALIDATE** - Acknowledge your feelings first
2. **EXPLORE** - Ask clarifying questions if needed
3. **SUPPORT** - Offer perspective or techniques
4. **EMPOWER** - End with something that builds your autonomy

|------|---------|--------------|

| **Advice** | "What should I do?" | Explores options while encouraging your wisdom |
| **Processing** | Complex feelings | Gentle questions to help you understand |
| **Celebrating** | Sharing wins | Genuine celebration without overdoing it |

### What Your Guide Knows

Your guide has access to **all your data** with exact timestamps:

| Data Type | What It Knows | Example Questions |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Tracking Logs** | Every tap with exact counts | "How many times did I exercise this month?" |

## Health Integration (Weather)

Your body often knows before your mind does. Heart rate spikes, poor sleep, and low activity all affect how you feel. Connecting HealthKit lets your guide:

- Notice when your heart rate spikes (stress indicator)
- Acknowledge poor sleep might affect your mood

When your heart rate spikes significantly:
1. Your phone buzzes with a gentle notification

- Your mood scores on good sleep nights

**Example insight:**
> "Looking at your data, you consistently feel better on days after 7+ hours of sleep."

### Health

**Does Mood Leaf diagnose health issues?**
Never. We describe what we observe ("heart rate elevated"), never diagnose ("panic attack").

**Is HealthKit required?**
No. Mood Leaf works great without it.
